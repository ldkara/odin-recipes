Mouth watering Sinigang for you, champ!

Every Filipino, and I mean EVERY Filipino, should have some degree of love for Sinigang. Some people are not really fond of them, such as my brother, but that only means they're weird. Sinigang is THE BEST hands down.... the best Filipino dish ever created. I heard that other countries have some kind of rendition on it such as Indonesia, which makes sense since we're kind of near each other, but for me this definitely represents the country. Filipinos love soupy dishes during rainy or stormy weather, or even just during any morning. This sour and umami-filled flavor-bomb of a soup dish will make you want to be Filipino.

There are different recipes for this, usually based on what meat you're using. There's the malapot kind of soup wherein the root crop gabi is used to thicken up the soup with its starchy goodness, and there's the more watery soup which I love; here's how the watery kind is made for the fish Bangus

Ingredients

  • 1 large milkfish (sliced into serving pieces)
  • 1 thumb ginger (sliced)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 4 medium tomatoes (sliced into chunks)
  • 1 bunch string beans (cut into shorter pieces)
  • 1 bunch kangkong
  • 1 small white radish (sliced)
  • 1 small eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 sachet Sinigang Mix fish sauce to taste
  • 3 – 4 green chilies
  • 4 – 5 cups of rice water (or plain water)

Instructions

  1. Wash and prepare the milkfish. Slice them into large serving pieces and transfer to a bowl. Sprinkle and rub some salt gently.
  2. Heat some oil in a pan. Fry the fish chunks lightly for a few minutes until half cooked. Transfer them to a plate and set aside.
  3. On the same pan, add the ginger, onion and tomatoes. Then, pour some rice water (or plain water). Cover the pot and wait for the broth to boil.
  4. Once boiling, add the string beans and sliced radish. Let this cook for 5 minutes. Then, add the okra and eggplant. Cook for 3 more minutes.
  5. Add the Sinigang Mix powder and stir to mix well. Season with some fish sauce to taste.
  6. Add the fried bangus slices into the broth. Make sure that all slices are submerged. Then, add the kangkong leaves. Stir gently to cook the kangkong leaves. Add the whole green chilies and cook for 3 to 5 more minutes.
  7. Once all ingredients are cooked, the dish is ready. Turn the heat off.
  8. Transfer the sinigang na bangus to a serving bowl. Enjoy it as a soup or have it with some hot steaming rice. Yum!
