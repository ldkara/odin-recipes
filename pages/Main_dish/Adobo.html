How to cook saucy and moist ADOBO

What better way to start your week than to stock up on your juicy, saucy, moist adobo!! Every Filipino family has their very own recipe on making their adobo. I wasn't really taught by my family on how to make adobo, it was more so from my own research how to make my own adobo - something that suits my taste of being tangy, salty, and garlicy! And I love my Adobo to be moist and SAUCY! Here's how I do it:

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kilo chicken thigh (any chicken part is ok, chicken thigh is always moist!)
  • 1 head of garlic
  • 1 head of onion
  • 50 mL of vinegar of choice (apple cider or cane vinegar is great)
  • 100 mL of soy sauce (can mix 50 mL dark soy sauce and 50 mL light soy sauce)
  • 1-2 tbsp brown sugar (white sugar is ok)
  • 1 tspn salt
  • Dash of pepper corns
  • 3 Bay leaves

Instructions

  1. Chop all your ingredients. Chicken thighs should be cubed, onions diced or sliced, garlic sliced (crushed is bettter to maximize the garlicy flavor). Divide garlic into 1/2 portions.
  2. Mix the marinade. In a bowl, mix 1/2 of crushed garlic, vinegar, soy sauce (dark and light), sugar, salt, pepper corns, and bay leaves
  3. Put chicken thigh parts cut earlier into the marinade and leave for 30 mins to 1 hour
  4. Heat pan at medium heat
  5. Saute garlic and onion. In the pan, saute your 2nd half of garlic set aside earlier until translucent. Then, add in your onions until caramelized.
  6. Pan fry your chicken thighs skin side down. After a few minutes, flip over to color the other side
  7. Pour in marinade into the pan and simmer for another 30 mins to 1 hour (longer makes it taste better!) or until sauce is reduced to your desired consistency
  8. Adjust to taste, then serve!
