Milo Ice Candy Everyday!

There was a time in my childhood where very few blocks of houses down the street would be selling some sort of flavored ice candy, which is just essentially frozen flavored milk ice pops. My most favority is the Milo Ice Candy that my cousin made us to last for a month!!! Here's one way to make them:

Ingredients

  • 1 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp tapioca flour, or cornstarch
  • 2-4 tbsp condensed milk
  • One 354 ml can evaporated milk
  • 3/4 cup Milo powder

Instructions

  1. Add water and tapioca/cornstarch to a saucepan and mix until combined.
  2. Turn on your stove to medium heat and whisk continuously. Once it begins to thicken, add evaporated milk, Milo powder, and condensed milk. Whisk to combine.
  3. Continue to cook on medium heat for 3-5 minutes while continuously whisking to avoid lumps.
  4. After the mixture has thickened, remove from heat and allow to come to room temperature.*
  5. Once the mixture comes to room temperature, fill ice candy bags or ice pop molds with a ladle and funnel.
  6. Place in freezer for at least 12 hours or until frozen. Enjoy!
